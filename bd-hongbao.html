The Hongbao Idea

A red envelope
of good fortune.

Hongbao takes its name from the red envelope offered at moments of celebration, a small gift that carries warmth, luck and intention.

We cook contemporary Chinese and East Asian cuisine with that same spirit: precise technique, generous hospitality and produce we are proud of. Cantonese roots meet Sichuan heat, Japanese restraint and the flavours of Dhaka, plated for the way the city dines today.

Kushal Lama
Executive Chef
I want every plate to feel like a red envelope: opened with anticipation, leaving you a little luckier than before.

Chef Kushal Lama leads the Hongbao kitchen with a quiet discipline shaped across the dim sum counters and live fire stations of Asia. His cooking honours the old grammar of Cantonese steaming and Sichuan spice, then lets the wood fire and the seasons of Bangladesh write the rest.

Doughs are rolled to order, stocks are simmered through the afternoon, and nothing reaches the pass that he has not tasted himself. It is fine dining without ceremony for its own sake: serious food, served generously.
