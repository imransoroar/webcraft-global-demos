From the Open Flame

Smoke, char and
centuries of fire.

Every kabob is grilled over a live charcoal fire, the heat catching the edges, the smoke folding into the marinade. Our chefs use centuries-old techniques carried from Kandahar to Mississauga, turning simple skewers into something you remember.

Naan is pulled fresh from the oven beside the grill, rice steamed and folded with whole spice. This is Afghan hospitality the way it was meant to be served, hot and generous.

Why the fire matters

The open flame is not a gimmick, it is the heart of Afghan cooking. It seals the juices, lifts the spice and gives every plate its signature char.

Live Charcoal
