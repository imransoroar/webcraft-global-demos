Made by hand

A bird that listens to wood. A studio that does the same.

Since 2016 we have grown from a single bench into a small studio of makers in Dhaka. We still cut, fit and finish each piece by hand, the slow way, because that is the only way to make furniture that feels alive in a room.

01

Drawn before it is cut

Every piece starts as a sketch and a full-scale story board, sized to your room and the way you sit, eat and rest.

02

Joined, not just glued

Mortise and tenon, dowel and dovetail. We choose joints that hold for decades, then reinforce by hand.

03

Upholstered in-house

Our sofas and lounge chairs are cushioned and stitched on site so the comfort matches the frame underneath.

04

Oiled, then rested

We finish with rubbed oils that deepen the grain, then let each piece rest before it ever leaves the studio.

Wood & materials

We start by choosing the tree, not the trend.

A woodpecker can tell a sound branch from a hollow one. We do the same with our timber, sourcing seasoned hardwood and reading each board for grain, weight and honesty before it ever meets a chisel.

Teak (Segun)Warm, water-resistant, ages to a deep gold
MahoganyRich red-brown with a tight, even grain
OakPale, strong, with an open expressive figure
Reclaimed hardwoodOld beams given a second, longer life

What honest making means here

  • Solid timber frames, no chipboard or hidden fillers
  • Hand-rubbed natural oils and waxes, low odour
  • Seasoned wood, dried to survive Dhaka humidity
  • Off-cuts turned into decor, vases and small pieces
  • A maker you can speak to, from sketch to delivery
