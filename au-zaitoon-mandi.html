The Art of Mandi

Smoke, patience, and a pit beneath the ground.

Mandi is not rushed. The name comes from the Arabic nada, meaning dew, for the gentle moisture that keeps the meat tender as it cooks. In Hadramawt, families have lowered seasoned lamb into a clay-lined pit for generations, sealing in heat and aromatic smoke.

We honour that method in Auburn. The result is rice that carries every note of the spice, and meat you lift apart with your fingers.

1

The Spice Grind

Cardamom, black lime, cumin and saffron are ground fresh each morning for our signature blend.

2

The Pit Smoke

Marinated lamb is hung above charcoal and rice, sealed so the smoke and dripping fat perfume every grain.

3

The Shared Platter

Served on one wide tray to be eaten together, because Mandi was always meant to gather people.
