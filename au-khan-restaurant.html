Slow-Cooked Tradition

Patience Is Our First Ingredient

Some dishes cannot be rushed. In our kitchen the heaviest pots sit on the lowest flame, sometimes overnight, until the spice and the meat become one.

Eight-Hour Nihari

Our signature nihari is simmered through the night with marrow bones and a wax-sealed potli of whole spices, the way Old Delhi breakfast houses have done it for centuries.

The Living Tandoor

A clay tandoor kept fiercely hot with charcoal sears naan in seconds and gives our tikka and tandoori their smoke, char and tenderness no oven can copy.

Dum Biryani

Rice and marinated meat are sealed under a tight lid and finished on dum, low steam, so every long grain drinks in saffron, kewra and the depth of the masala.
